Understanding Cutting Saddles

The Cutting Saddle Difference

A cutting saddle is the most specialized western saddle built — deep seat, high cantle, tall horn, dropped rigging. Every feature exists because of NCHA's free-rein rule: once a competitor drops the reins, the saddle must hold them passive and still through the violent lateral moves a cutting horse makes following a cow. A Teddy Johnson with documented NCHA competition history is a proven tool. The $30,000 AMT designation on our Teddy Johnson tells you exactly where it has been.
