Superior Saddlery — Andy Maschke
Greenville, Texas  ·  Active Production
ReiningCow HorseRanchCutting Active Production

Andy Maschke founded Superior Saddlery after training as a saddlemaker in Europe before relocating to Arizona and later establishing the shop in Greenville, Texas. Superior has grown into one of the most respected performance saddle makers in the western industry, building competition saddles for NRHA reining, NRCHA cow horse, AQHA Ranch Riding, and cutting disciplines. The shop's defining technical contribution to the industry is the SYMMETREES system — hardwood tree cores fully encapsulated in fiberglass — which eliminates the moisture warping that permanently alters the fit profile of traditional rawhide-covered wood trees. A Superior saddle maintains its tree geometry and horse fit season after season regardless of climate variation, humidity changes, or extended storage. This structural consistency is the primary reason used Superior saddles hold their resale value better than most comparable-priced makers.

Superior builds each discipline on trees purpose-designed for that specific event, not adapted from a shared platform. The reining builds carry the geometry that active NRHA Futurity competitors have refined through decades of open competition. The cow horse builds address all three phases of NRCHA competition — reining pattern, fence work, and boxing — in a single saddle designed to perform correctly in each phase. The Ranch Rider and Ranch Rider Deluxe lines are purpose-built for AQHA Ranch Riding competition, not reining saddles with back cinches added as an afterthought.

The signature model program is built around working collaborations with active NRHA competitors including Casey Deary, Craig Schmersal, Jason Vanlandingham, Mandy McCutcheon, and others. These are genuine design partnerships in which the competitor's specific preferences shape the seat geometry, horn dimensions, and rigging position of each model. Each signature saddle carries the competitor's name stamped on the fender alongside the Superior mark and is verifiable through that mark. New Superior Saddlery saddles are available through David Solum as part of the Certified Saddle Network.

Donn Leson
Grants Pass, Oregon  ·  Used Market Only — Deceased
Reining Used Market Only — Deceased

Donn Leson built reining saddles in Grants Pass, Oregon with a devoted following among NRHA open competitors that grew steadily through the 1980s and 1990s as his reputation spread through the professional reining community. He is no longer living and production has permanently ceased, making his saddles available only through the used market. Despite the passage of time, Leson's Reinmaker remains the most sought-after used reining saddle in the market — clean examples consistently hold value at $7,000 to $8,500 because no current production saddle has replicated the specific combination of seat geometry, tree construction, and finish quality that he achieved.

Leson's defining characteristic was an exceptionally flat, precisely balanced seat combined with a tree geometry that positioned the rider optimally for the sliding stop without restricting movement through circles, lead changes, or spins. The seat leather quality on his saddles is consistent and ages well — examples from the 1990s that have been properly maintained remain fully rideable and competitive. The TVB variant, built on a modified tree to a custom specification, commands a further premium above the standard Reinmaker in the used market and appears less frequently.

Authentication of Donn Leson saddles is straightforward for anyone familiar with his work. The fender stamp, tooling execution, seat construction, and overall refinement of finish are consistent identifiers. The serial number system ties back to his production records. Leson saddles are not commonly counterfeited — the construction quality required to produce a convincing fake exceeds what most production shops can deliver. When a genuine Reinmaker or TVB surfaces in the used market at honest pricing it does not remain available long. David Solum has bought and sold Donn Leson saddles for over 40 years and can authenticate any example on request.

Dale Harwood — Trail's End Saddlery
Shelley, Idaho  ·  Retired 2018 — Collector Market
RanchRopingWorking Retired 2018 — Collector Market

Dale Harwood is the most decorated American saddlemaker of the 20th century — a recipient of a National Heritage Fellowship from the National Endowment for the Arts, the highest lifetime honor presented to a master traditional artist in the United States. He opened his first saddle shop in Idaho Falls, Idaho in 1961 after years of working as a buckaroo and rodeo contestant throughout the region, an experience that gave him a practical understanding of what made a saddle work in hard country. He later moved his shop to his rural home in Shelley and built the last two saddles of his career in 2018, formally retiring after more than five decades of production.

Every step of Harwood's process was performed entirely by hand. He made his own wooden saddle trees from scratch, processed his own rawhide, hand-cut and hand-stamped the leather, applied it to the tree himself, and designed and engraved the gold and silver finishes that characterized his finest work. His tooling — particularly his floral work, which he designed to remain as close to nature as possible — is recognized among saddlemakers and collectors as among the finest executed by any American craftsman in the modern era. Cowboy artist Joe Beeler described Harwood's approach as constructing a saddle and then giving it life.

Harwood saddles are collector items as much as working tools. Clean examples trade at $7,000 to well over $25,000 at auction depending on tooling complexity, silver work, and provenance — a Harwood saddle documented as belonging to Ray Hunt has sold at auction. These are generational pieces that serious collectors and Western art institutions pursue actively. When a Harwood surfaces in the used market it is an event in the saddlemaking and working cowboy communities, not a routine transaction.

Kyle Tack
Weatherford, Texas  ·  Active Production
ReiningCow Horse Active Production

Kyle Tack has built reining and cow horse saddles out of Weatherford, Texas for several decades, operating in the same geographic center of the western performance saddle industry that houses Bob's Custom, Martin Saddlery, and the broader ecosystem of performance saddlemakers that has made Weatherford the acknowledged capital of American reining saddle production. The shop produces saddles across a range of price points, all built on trees with a consistent geometry and horse fit that has been proven across decades of NRHA and NRCHA competition at the non-pro, amateur, and open levels.

Kyle Tack saddles are widely and consistently regarded as the most undervalued reining saddles in the used market. The leather quality — typically Hermann Oak skirting — is high and consistent across the production run. The tree geometry positions the rider correctly for the sliding stop and the circular patterns without the compromises that affect saddles built to a lower standard or a tighter budget. Clean Kyle Tack reining saddles in good to excellent condition regularly appear in the used market at $3,500 to $4,500, a range that puts them in direct competition with saddles priced significantly higher at retail by other makers.

The shop produces both standard production runs and limited or custom builds. Standard builds are identified by model numbers on the fender stamp; custom and limited builds carry additional identifying marks. Authentication is straightforward — the Kyle Tack maker's stamp is clearly placed and consistently applied across all production years. For a buyer entering NRHA competition at the non-pro or amateur level who needs a proven, correctly built reining saddle without paying top-of-market prices, a clean used Kyle Tack represents the strongest value proposition in the used performance saddle market.

Teddy Johnson
Gainesville, Texas  ·  Semi-Retired — Used Market Primary
Cutting Semi-Retired — Used Market Primary

Teddy Johnson built cutting saddles in Gainesville, Texas for decades with a reputation built entirely within the National Cutting Horse Association competition community. Johnson is semi-retired and active production is minimal, making his saddles available primarily through the used market. His cutting saddles are defined by a correctly executed dropped rigging position, a properly proportioned deep seat suited to the violent lateral movements of a working cutting horse, and leather quality that consistently outlasts comparable-era production saddles from makers building at similar or higher price points.

The dropped rigging on a Teddy Johnson cutting saddle is not cosmetic — it is a functional specification that pulls the cinch back from the horse's shoulder and allows maximum front-end freedom during the explosive lateral work that defines cutting competition. The deep seat geometry keeps the rider centered and passive through the horse's movements without requiring them to grip or brace, which is critical in a discipline where the free-rein rule prohibits touching the reins once the cow is committed. His horn dimensions and placement are correct for NCHA competition use — present and functional as a bracing point without interfering with the rider's natural hand position.

Johnson saddles appear regularly in NCHA competition at all levels from amateur through open. The provenance of a saddle with documented NCHA competition history carries meaning for buyers within the cutting community. Used Teddy Johnson cutting saddles in functional condition in the $900 to $2,500 range represent strong value for a serious NCHA competitor. The construction holds up consistently well — it is not uncommon to find 20-year-old examples that remain fully rideable and competitive with basic maintenance. David Solum has current Teddy Johnson cutting saddles in inventory at prices that reflect their honest market value.

Calvin Allen Saddlery
Weatherford, Texas  ·  Active Production
CuttingRanch Active Production

Calvin Allen operates out of Weatherford, Texas and builds cutting and crossover saddles with a following that spans the National Cutting Horse Association competition community and the AQHA Ranch Cutting market. Allen has decades of experience building for the NCHA non-pro and amateur market, and his saddles are well-regarded within the cutting community for honest construction, correct geometry, and pricing that makes quality cutting saddle design accessible to competitors who are not operating at the professional level.

Allen's Ranch Cutter model occupies a practical and well-defined position in the western saddle market — a saddle that incorporates the deep seat and dropped rigging geometry of a purpose-built cutting saddle with a more substantial horn and rigging system suited to light ranch work and cattle sorting. The Ranch Cutter addresses riders who compete in NCHA cutting or AQHA Ranch Cutting events but also use their horse for everyday ranch work where a pure competition cutting saddle would be inadequate. This crossover positioning fills a genuine market need that neither a pure cutting saddle nor a pure ranch saddle addresses correctly.

Standard Calvin Allen cutting builds are well-proportioned competition saddles with correct tree geometry, proper rigging placement for NCHA cutting requirements, and construction quality that holds up through sustained competition use. Trees are sound on examples from all production years. Used Calvin Allen saddles hold their value reasonably well relative to their original price points. For a rider entering cutting competition at the non-pro or amateur level, or competing in AQHA Ranch Cutting events, a clean used Calvin Allen represents honest competition equipment at a price point that respects the buyer's budget without compromising the fundamental design requirements of the discipline.

Martin Saddlery
Greenville, Texas  ·  Active Production
ReiningCow HorseRanch Active Production

Martin Saddlery is based in Greenville, Texas and has produced competition saddles for the reining, cow horse, and ranch riding markets for several decades. The shop operates a signature model program with several active NRHA and NRCHA competitors, and their builds appear in both open and non-pro competition at major NRHA and NRCHA events. Martin occupies a solid position in the mid-to-upper performance market, with new saddles running $3,500 to $6,500 depending on model, silver package, and custom specifications.

The Trevor Dare signature reining build is among the most recognized Martin Saddlery models in current production, carrying a premium above standard production lines. Martin's reining builds are well-regarded in the broader NRHA non-pro and amateur competition market for fit across a diverse range of horse types — an important practical consideration for non-professional competitors who may not have the same consistency of horse conformation that open competitors maintain. The tree geometry on Martin's reining saddles positions the rider correctly for the stop and the circular patterns without the compromises that affect lower-budget production.

In the AQHA Ranch Riding market, Martin Saddlery has an established and growing presence. Their ranch builds are purpose-designed for the specific requirements of AQHA Ranch Riding, Ranch Trail, and Ranch Reining competition — correctly proportioned for the long, vertical leg position that judges reward in ranch horse competition. Martin Saddlery is a significant supplier to the non-pro and amateur competition market across both NRHA and AQHA. Used Martin saddles in good to excellent condition hold their value reasonably well, particularly the signature builds, and represent honest competition equipment at accessible prices in the used market.

Roohide
Western Performance Market  ·  Active Production
ReiningCow Horse Active Production

Roohide is a production saddle label that has supplied the NRHA competition market for several decades, targeting the non-pro and amateur reining competitor who needs a purpose-built reining saddle without the pricing of the top custom makers. The brand covers a range of price points from mid-market upward and has been ridden in NRHA non-pro and amateur competition at regional and national events throughout its production history. Roohide saddles appear consistently in the used performance saddle market and represent a practical entry point for riders who are stepping into organized reining competition for the first time.

Construction quality on Roohide production saddles is generally consistent within each model line. The trees are adequate for non-pro competition use — they are not built to the same specification as the trees in a Superior or Kyle Tack, but they are functional reining saddle trees that will perform correctly for an amateur competitor riding at regional levels. The seat geometry is a reining design with a flat seat and forward-balanced stirrup position. Leather quality runs mid-range — serviceable and appropriate to the price point without the premium Hermann Oak skirting that characterizes top-tier custom saddles.

In the used market, Roohide saddles in good condition appear regularly at the $1,500 to $3,500 price range. They are not sought-after collector saddles and do not carry the resale premiums of Kyle Tack, Superior, or Donn Leson — but honest examples in good functional condition are legitimate competition saddles for a rider who needs to compete at the non-pro or amateur level without a major capital investment. For a youth competitor or an adult amateur entering the NRHA for the first time, a clean used Roohide at fair pricing is a practical and reasonable starting point before committing to a premium build.

Pinnacle Saddlery — Andreas Maschke
Arizona  ·  Ceased — Earlier Brand
Reining Ceased — Earlier Brand

Pinnacle Saddlery was an earlier brand under which Andreas Maschke built saddles before establishing Superior Saddlery in Greenville, Texas. Pinnacle saddles were built in Arizona and represent Maschke's earlier production work, before the development of the SYMMETREES fiberglass-encapsulated tree technology that defines Superior's current lineup. The brand is no longer in active production and saddles are available only through the used market, where they appear infrequently given the limited production volume during the Pinnacle years.

Pinnacle saddles are characterized by exceptionally precise and consistent tooling execution that distinguishes them immediately from production saddles of the same era. The daisy and sunflower floral patterns on Pinnacle builds are carved with a tightness and consistency of depth that reflects Maschke's European saddlemaking training — a level of tooling precision that is verifiable by anyone familiar with his work across both the Pinnacle and Superior production years. The AF Reiner model in full floral coverage with a major sterling silver package represents the premium end of Pinnacle production and the most visually impressive examples that appear in the used market.

Pinnacle saddles are uncommon in the used performance saddle market. When a clean full-floral example with a substantial silver package surfaces it attracts both collectors who recognize Maschke's craftsmanship and competitive riders who understand that the construction quality of a Pinnacle saddle is consistent with what Superior Saddlery produces today. Authentication is verifiable through the maker's stamp, tooling execution, and silver quality — factors that are consistent and recognizable across all Pinnacle production. These are not Superior Saddlery saddles in the current SYMMETREES construction, but they share the same maker and the same foundational commitment to quality and fit.

Tier 2 — Mid-Range American Production
Circle Y
Yoakum, Texas  ·  Active — Est. 1960
RanchReiningBarrelTrailShow Active — Est. 1960

Circle Y was founded by Leland Tucker in Yoakum, Texas in 1960 — a small town two hours east of San Antonio that has produced a disproportionate share of the American western saddle industry. Tucker built the company from scratch with a stated objective to produce finer products than the saddle industry was then offering, and Circle Y grew over the following decades into one of the largest American saddle manufacturers by volume. The company remained family-operated until 2000, after which it passed to new ownership while maintaining its Yoakum base and manufacturing operations.

Circle Y produces across virtually every western riding discipline — ranch, reining, barrel racing, trail, show, roping, cutting, and youth. Their patented Flex2 tree design accommodates a broader range of horse conformations than a traditional rigid tree by allowing controlled flex in the bars, which has made the brand popular in the all-around, trail, and AQHA ranch event market where riders encounter diverse horse types that a single rigid tree cannot fit universally. Circle Y is widely distributed through authorized dealers across the United States, Canada, and internationally.

Circle Y occupies the mid-market at $800 to $2,500 new depending on model and discipline. They are not competition-performance saddles in the sense of a Superior Saddlery or Kyle Tack custom build, but they are honest American-made production saddles at prices accessible to the working ranch, recreational, and amateur competition market. In the AQHA Ranch Riding market specifically, Circle Y has an established and growing presence with models purpose-designed for the ranch horse competition format. Used Circle Y saddles hold moderate value — functional, reliable, and widely available, but not collectible in the way that top-tier custom makers are.

Genuine Billy Cook
Sulphur, Oklahoma  ·  Active — Est. early 1950s
RanchRopingReiningBarrelTrail Active — Est. early 1950s

Billy Cook began building saddles in Greenville, Texas in the early 1950s before partnering with Potts-Longhorn from 1953 to 1988, then relocating the operation permanently to Sulphur, Oklahoma. The company is now owned by Adam Trenk, an Arizona horseman and attorney who has maintained the brand's commitment to American construction — real American leather, rawhide-covered wooden trees, and hand assembly in Sulphur — at mid-market price points accessible to working horsemen. The commitment to domestic materials and handmade construction has defined the brand throughout its history and continues under current ownership.

The Billy Cook lineup covers roping, ranch, reining, barrel racing, and trail disciplines at new prices ranging from $1,200 to $3,000 depending on model and specifications. The roping and ranch builds are the core of the brand's reputation — stout, properly rigged saddles built for cattle work, team roping, and the sustained hard use of a working ranch operation. The rigging on Billy Cook working saddles is full double with back cinch as standard on ranch and roping builds, correctly configured for rope pressure and cattle work in the way that working cowboys require.

Billy Cook saddles are among the most durable mid-market American saddles available — a consequence of the rawhide-covered wooden tree construction and the attention to material quality that the shop has maintained consistently throughout its history. They are not NRHA open competition-grade saddles and are not represented at major NRHA Futurity events in open competition, but they have a strong and loyal following among working cowboys, team ropers, and ranch hands who have ridden them for decades and returned to the brand repeatedly because the saddles hold together under conditions that lower-quality production saddles do not survive. Used Billy Cook saddles in good condition hold their value well in the working ranch market.

Billy Royal
United States  ·  Active Production
ShowReiningRanchWork Active Production

Billy Royal produces western show, reining, and work saddles with a focus on the AQHA and breed show market. The brand offers a range of western saddle styles including western pleasure show saddles, reining builds, and working saddles across disciplines including breeds ranging from Arabian to Quarter Horse. Billy Royal trees carry a lifetime guarantee — a commitment that reflects the brand's confidence in their tree construction and differentiates them from production makers who do not offer similar guarantees on the structural component that determines a saddle's long-term value.

The show saddle line features detailed hand-tooling with silver work appropriate to the western pleasure and equitation show market. The reining builds incorporate a close-contact seat design with pre-turned fenders that allow a correct leg position from the first ride without the break-in period that new saddles with unturned fenders require. Work saddles in the Billy Royal lineup are built on the same custom wooden trees as the show saddles, offering riders who need a functional working saddle the same structural foundation as the higher-finish competition builds.

Billy Royal occupies a solid position in the mid-tier show and pleasure market, with pricing running $1,500 to $3,500 new depending on silver content and model. They are less prominent in NRHA open and non-pro competition circles than the Weatherford, Texas custom makers, but are well-regarded among amateur AQHA exhibitors across the pleasure, equitation, and ranch riding disciplines. Used Billy Royal saddles in good condition hold their value in the AQHA amateur and breed show market. For a rider competing in AQHA western pleasure, horsemanship, or ranch riding at the amateur level who needs a correctly finished show saddle at mid-market pricing, Billy Royal represents a practical and reliable option.

Tier 3 — Factory & Volume Production
Tex Tan / American Saddlery
Yoakum, Texas  ·  Active — High Volume Production
RanchTrailRopingPleasure Active — High Volume Production

Tex Tan, operating as part of the American Saddlery group and based in Yoakum, Texas — the same small town that is home to Circle Y and a concentration of other saddle manufacturers — is one of the higher-volume western saddle producers in the United States. Tex Tan produces entry-level to mid-range western saddles across ranch, trail, roping, and pleasure disciplines at price points accessible to the entry-level and recreational buyer. New prices run $400 to $1,400 depending on model and specifications.

Tex Tan saddles are built using American leather and domestic production, which distinguishes them from import brands that source construction overseas. The ranch and roping builds in the Tex Tan lineup carry basic working rigging adequate for light ranch duties and recreational roping. Trail and pleasure models are constructed for comfort at accessible price points. The construction standard is appropriate to the price range — not the premium Hermann Oak leather and hand-stamped tooling of a Kyle Tack or Bob's Custom, but honest domestic production at prices that entry-level buyers can sustain.

In the used market, Tex Tan saddles in good condition typically sell in the $200 to $600 range — a reflection of their original market position as accessible, practical saddles rather than collectible or competition-performance equipment. These are not saddles that appear in NRHA, NCHA, or NRCHA competition. They serve the trail rider, the recreational ranch hand, the beginning rider learning the fundamentals, and the buyer who needs a functional western saddle at the lowest sustainable price point. For that buyer, Tex Tan provides honest American-made construction without the markup of premium brands.

Reinsman
Production — Western Performance Market  ·  Active Production
ReiningRanchTrail Active Production

Reinsman produces mid-market western saddles marketed primarily to the reining and ranch disciplines, along with an extensive line of saddle pads, bits, and other equine equipment that has broader market recognition than their saddle production. The saddle line runs $800 to $2,000 new and is positioned as accessible reining and ranch saddle equipment for the amateur and recreational rider who wants a purpose-built western performance saddle without the price of a custom maker. Reinsman has a consistent presence in the youth, novice, and limited amateur reining market.

Reinsman reining saddles carry flat seat geometry, minimal horn, and in-skirt rigging in the style of the competition reining market. The construction is factory production — consistent within each model line, appropriate to the price point, and suited to the non-professional competitor riding at regional and local NRHA events. These are not saddles that appear at the NRHA Futurity or major NRCHA events in open competition, but they perform adequately for amateur and youth competitors at local and regional levels where the competition standard does not demand the geometry of a premium custom build.

In the used market, Reinsman saddles in good condition sell in the $400 to $900 range. They represent practical entry-level competition equipment for youth and novice riders stepping into organized reining competition for the first time. The Reinsman pad and bit lines are more widely used across performance disciplines at all levels than the saddle line — a useful reminder that a brand's reputation in one product category does not necessarily transfer to another. For a beginning reining competitor on a limited budget who needs functional equipment to learn the discipline, a clean used Reinsman at fair pricing is a practical and honest starting point.

Tucker Saddlery
United States  ·  Active Production
TrailEnduranceGaited Active Production

Tucker Saddlery specializes in trail, endurance, and gaited horse saddles built around a proprietary seat design focused on long-distance comfort for the rider over sustained hours of riding. Tucker produces American-made saddles at mid-range prices from approximately $800 to $2,000 new. The saddle design philosophy centers on rider biomechanics across long-distance riding rather than arena performance — the seat geometry, stirrup position, and padding systems are engineered for an all-day ride in varied terrain rather than the precision of a sliding stop or the athleticism of cutting cattle.

Tucker saddles are widely used in trail riding, distance riding, endurance racing, and gaited horse disciplines where their design priorities are most relevant. The brand has a strong and loyal following among serious trail riders and endurance competitors who prioritize sustainable comfort over many hours in the saddle. Gaited horse riders in particular have found Tucker saddles compatible with the movement patterns of gaited breeds that can be problematic for traditional western saddle designs. The construction is American-made with attention to the durability requirements of extended outdoor use across varying terrain and weather conditions.

Tucker Saddlery is not a factor in NRHA reining, NCHA cutting, NRCHA cow horse, or AQHA Ranch Riding competition markets — these are fundamentally different use cases with different design requirements, and Tucker does not attempt to bridge them. In the used market, Tucker saddles in good condition sell among trail and endurance buyers at prices that reflect their specialized purpose. For a rider whose primary use is trail riding, distance riding, or gaited horse work who prioritizes all-day comfort above arena performance, Tucker represents a purpose-built option from a maker with a genuine understanding of that market's specific requirements.

Bob's Custom Saddles
Weatherford, Texas  ·  Active Production — Est. 1970s
ReiningCow HorseCutting Active Production — Est. 1970s

Bob's Custom Saddles out of Weatherford, Texas is the most represented maker in western performance saddle inventories nationally and has accumulated a competition record that no other maker has matched — 16 NRHA Futurity winners, 24 of 35 NRCHA Snaffle Bit Futurity winners, and 11 of 18 World's Greatest Horseman winners by the company's own documentation. Bob Avila, Casey Deary, Craig Schmersal, Jason Vanlandingham, Mandy McCutcheon, and dozens of other NRHA and NRCHA professionals have ridden Bob's Custom in Futurity and Derby competition. The saddles are also represented at the NCHA Futurity and the World Equestrian Games, across AQHA world championships in reining, working cowhorse, cutting, western riding, horsemanship, and trail, and at the Wrangler National Finals Rodeo.

The model range is broad. Standard production builds carry alphanumeric model numbers — B16-1455M, B14-867M, B99-472, and others — and are honest production saddles built to a consistent specification using Hermann Oak leather with stainless steel hardware and real sheepskin lining. Signature builds bearing a competitor's name are a step above in tooling execution, silver content, and finish quality. The Bob Avila signature line is the most sought-after in the used market; genuine examples carry both the "Bob Avila" and "Bob's Custom" stamps on the fender, with a serial number on the tree cap traceable to production records. Any seller who claims a "Bob Avila style" saddle without the fender stamps is not offering a genuine signature build.

The KR Lady Reiner is Bob's Custom's purpose-designed women's competition saddle — narrower twist, shorter seat, and build geometry specifically suited to the female competitive reiner. These are consistently underpriced in the used market relative to their construction quality and represent strong value for a woman competing in NRHA non-pro or amateur events. The Lady Cowhorse is a purpose-designed NRCHA crossover build with tree geometry suited to all three phases of reined cow horse competition — reining pattern, fence work, and boxing — and is available in both used and brand new builds through David Solum's inventory.

Bob's Custom trees are generally sound even in heavily used examples — the shop builds a stout tree to a consistent specification across all production years. The areas most likely to show wear in older production models are the seat jockeys, which can crack at the stitching seam on older builds, and the silver swell caps on high-end signature builds, which occasionally work loose from extended use. Both issues are addressable by a competent saddle repair shop. Neither is a deal-breaker when the purchase price accurately reflects the condition of those components.
